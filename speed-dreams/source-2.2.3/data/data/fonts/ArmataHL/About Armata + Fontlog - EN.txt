Armata is a new Sans-Serif Font by Jasper @ Cannot Into Space Fonts.

It is named after the new Russian T-14 Armata Main Battle Tank.

Version History

v0.9.7:

Initial Release.

Includes basic Russian Cyrillic and basic Latin characters, started work on Latin-1 Supplement, Latin Extended A & B, IPA Extensions, Greek, and the rest of the Cyrillic and Cyrillic Supplement sets.

Includes Russian Ruble, Euro, and Ukrainian Hryvnia signs.

413 Characters completed in Unicode BMP.

Only has Hairline weight, plus matching Oblique Italic.
